# Shaun Tan -> 超现实无字叙事视觉系统

## Use When

Use for migration, loneliness, cities, strange machines, wordless sequences, social allegory, memory, displacement, and stories where the world itself should feel unfamiliar.

## Distilled Visual Logic

- The world should feel almost recognizable, then gently wrong.
- Objects carry metaphor: suitcase, bird, machine, door, map, seed, window.
- Silence can be narrative.
- Architecture and scale can express emotion.
- The viewer should infer meaning, not be told everything.

## Page Grammar

- Cinematic framing and sequential visual clues.
- Muted palettes, sepia, gray, dusty color, sudden symbolic accents.
- Strange but believable objects.
- Small figures in heavy environments.
- Page turns reveal information like a silent film.

## Prompt Atoms

```text
surreal silent picture-book world, cinematic framing, small figure in vast city,
strange believable objects, muted sepia and dusty color, visual metaphor,
wordless sequence, displacement, quiet social allegory
```

## Avoid

- Do not say "in the style of Shaun Tan".
- Do not copy The Arrival's creature, city, or panel language directly.
- Do not make surrealism random; every strange object needs story function.
- Do not use this for simple preschool recognition books.
