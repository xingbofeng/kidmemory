# Maurice Sendak -> 儿童心理梦境线描视觉系统

## Use When

Use for fear, anger, wildness, bedtime, inner monsters, dream islands, emotional transformation, or stories where a child's forbidden feeling needs a visible body.

## Distilled Visual Logic

- The picture externalizes inner life.
- Crosshatching and line density create emotional weather.
- Monsters should feel psychologically true, not merely scary.
- A room can become a forest; a feeling can become a place.
- The child remains central even when the world becomes wild.

## Page Grammar

- Pen-and-ink structure with textured color.
- Dense hatching around emotional pressure.
- Dramatic silhouettes and theatrical space.
- Page turns should feel like crossing a threshold.
- Wildness must return to a human emotional need.

## Prompt Atoms

```text
expressive ink crosshatching, dreamlike child psychology, moonlit room,
wild forest shapes, emotional monsters, dense line texture, theatrical shadows,
bedtime unease, warm unruly energy, inner feeling made visible
```

## Avoid

- Do not say "in the style of Maurice Sendak".
- Do not copy iconic monster silhouettes or bedroom/forest compositions.
- Do not make horror. Keep it child-safe and emotionally legible.
- Do not use monsters without a psychological reason.
