# Lois Ehlert -> 图形自然拼贴视觉系统

## Use When

Use for plants, seeds, gardens, food, seasons, insects, birds, nature collections, and early-childhood nonfiction with strong graphic order.

## Distilled Visual Logic

- Make nature feel like it was collected, sorted, and arranged by curious hands.
- Shape and labeling logic matter, even if labels are added outside the image.
- High contrast and flat layout help children compare forms.
- Collage can feel like a classroom table full of leaves, shells, scraps, and seeds.

## Page Grammar

- Flat graphic composition.
- Cut paper, found texture, plant silhouettes.
- Strong color fields, clean edges, clear categories.
- Objects often face the viewer like specimens.
- Use repetition for comparison.

## Prompt Atoms

```text
graphic nature collage, cut paper plant shapes, seed and leaf forms,
flat lay composition, bold color fields, specimen arrangement,
early learning clarity, found-paper texture, compare-and-sort rhythm
```

## Avoid

- Do not say "in the style of Lois Ehlert".
- Do not copy book-specific layouts or object collections.
- Do not make nature too realistic; keep graphic clarity.
- Do not use this for emotionally heavy myth pages unless intentionally simplified.
