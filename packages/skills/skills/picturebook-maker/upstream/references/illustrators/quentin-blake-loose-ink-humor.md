# Quentin Blake -> 松弛墨线幽默视觉系统

## Use When

Use for mischievous children, comic timing, school chaos, fast action, expressive dialogue, and stories that need movement more than polish.

## Distilled Visual Logic

- The line should feel alive, not corrected to death.
- Gesture carries personality.
- Imperfect proportions can make a character more human.
- Watercolor washes should support speed and comedy.
- A page can feel as if the idea just ran across it.

## Page Grammar

- Loose scratchy ink line.
- Quick watercolor patches.
- Exaggerated body angles and facial gestures.
- Sparse background; action leads.
- Let characters lean, wobble, stretch, trip, point, protest.

## Prompt Atoms

```text
loose energetic ink line, quick watercolor wash, comic gesture,
imperfect proportions, expressive movement, mischievous child energy,
sparse background, fast readable action, lively page rhythm
```

## Avoid

- Do not say "in the style of Quentin Blake".
- Do not copy specific character faces from famous books.
- Do not over-polish the line.
- Do not use this when the story needs solemn mythic stillness.
