# 熊亮 -> 当代中国神话舞台视觉系统

## Use When

Use for Chinese myths, ghosts, spirits, mountain-sea stories, theatrical legends, origin stories, festival tales, and children-facing retellings that need Chinese atmosphere with contemporary clarity.

## Distilled Visual Logic

- Make the myth feel staged, not museum-like.
- Ink, shadow, folk theater, and symbolic landscape can carry narrative.
- Figures may be stylized, but their silhouette must be readable.
- Chinese motifs should be structural: clouds, mountains, waves, masks, robes, gates.
- A page can feel ancient and modern at once.

## Page Grammar

- Stage-like compositions with strong silhouettes.
- Ink wash, paper texture, shadow-puppet feeling, folk color accents.
- Big symbolic scenery: mountain, river, moon, gate, cloud.
- Characters stand like performers in a myth theater.
- Use mystery, but keep children oriented.

## Prompt Atoms

```text
contemporary Chinese myth picture-book stage, ink wash, paper texture,
shadow-puppet silhouette, symbolic mountains and clouds, theatrical myth,
folk color accents, ancient-modern atmosphere, child-readable staging
```

## Avoid

- Do not say "in the style of Xiong Liang".
- Do not copy named characters or book compositions.
- Do not make the page so mysterious that the story action disappears.
- Do not use random Chinese motifs without narrative function.
